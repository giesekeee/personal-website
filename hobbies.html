Some of my hobbies:

Below is a list of some of my hobbies in order of relevance:

  • Videogames
  • Music
  • Coding

Speaking a little more about these hobbies:

Videogames

Videogames have been a big hobby of mine for as long as I can remember. Way back when I had my Nintendo DS and my Playstation 3 where I would play games like Super Mario Bros and Grand Theft Auto V for hours and hours after school and over the summer. This, for me, and also for a lot of people similar to me, is where I met most of my friends in my younger years. I have known some of these guys for nearly 8 years at this point. Videogames is also what I turn to when I need to relieve stress. It has been quite enjoyable for my entire life and I would do it all over again if I could.

Music*

Music for me is one of the biggest facets of my life. While I do not personally make music, I have played instruments in the past and I have spent probably more time listening to music in my life than I have spent sleeping. I listen to a smorgasborg of genres stretching from Rock, to Pop, to HipHop, and sometimes even Classical. I constantly genre hop every month or so and I will play that specific genre or artist on repeat until something else catches my ear. Very similar to videogames, this has been a large stress reliever for me and I would even love to attempt making some of my own music in the future, even if I don't post it publicly.

Coding*

Coding for me has come up a lot more recent than a lot of my hobbies, but it has taken over my life at this point. I went from majoring in Mathematics and Science studying to be an engineer, to majoring in Arts studying to be a Web Developer. This hobby of mine came up during my senior year of high school, where over the summer prior an email went out to the students that mentioned a group of new incoming classes that weren't shown the previous spring when you were able to sign up for your classes. One of those new classes happened to be a Computer Science course taught by my favorite teacher. This was some of the most fun I have had during my high school education and I enjoyed it enough to where it ended up shifting my career direction. I want to make this hobby into a career sometime in the near future, and hopefully I enjoy it as much as I do now.

Art

While I may not contribute as much as I used to when it comes to art, I still do enjoy drawing from time to time or scrolling on my phone looking at different forms of art that have been made by professional painters, or animators, or digital artists. I grew up a very artsy person, and my grandmother for a long time kept on bothering me and teasing me because I "was such a good artist when you were younger, why did you stop?" I don't exactly have a reason as to why I stopped, but I'm not really a creative type of person. It takes a lot to get me to think of something to draw, and then with me being a perfectionist, I get really upset if it does not live up to my expectations. I have very few art pieces that I am proud of, fewer of which I have physical copies of.
